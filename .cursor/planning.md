What location are you building this guide for, and why did you choose it?

I'm building this guide for Rio de Janiero, Brazil (Rio). I chose 
this place because I've never been to South
America and Rio has always been a place 
I've wanted to go too. The combination of a 
different culture, close beaches, and laid-back
lifestyle has always been appealing to me 
and I've been wanting to explore it. 


Who is your primary audience? (e.g., first-time visitors, backpackers, families, local residents)

My primary audience would be young travelers (20s - 30s) who want to experience everything that Rio has to offer. This
includes its world famous beaches, food, nightlife 
and iconic monuments. 


What do you want visitors to feel or know after spending 5 minutes on your site?

I want them to feel like Rio is a place 
they have got to go to and experince once. They'll realize how much they've been missing out on and how great the food and culture is.

Are there any sections where the mobile experience should feel meaningfully different from desktop, not just smaller?

Yes, the navigation bar should feel different on mobile. Instead of just shrinking the horizontal links, they stack vertically and could potentially use a hamburger menu icon in the future for better mobile UX. The mobile navigation should be easy to tap with thumbs, so links need more vertical spacing (padding) than on desktop.

Also, the hero section on mobile should have the heading text sized appropriately so it doesn't overwhelm the small screen. The text should remain readable without users having to zoom in, which means reducing font sizes and adjusting padding.

The food guide and attractions pages on mobile feel fundamentally different because users scroll through one entry at a time (vertically) rather than scanning side-by-side content. This creates a more story-like, immersive experience where each dish or attraction gets full attention.
