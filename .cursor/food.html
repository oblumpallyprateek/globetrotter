Rio de Janeiro Food Guide

From savory street snacks to hearty traditional dishes, Rio's food scene is as diverse and vibrant as the city itself. Here are three must-try dishes that capture the authentic flavors of Brazilian cuisine.

Pão de Queijo

Perfect for Families

Casa do Pão de Queijo, Rua Gonçalves Dias, 32, Centro

These warm, cheesy bread balls are a beloved Brazilian snack that everyone loves. Made with tapioca flour and cheese, they're naturally gluten-free and irresistibly delicious. Crispy on the outside, soft and gooey on the inside—perfect for breakfast, a snack, or anytime you need a taste of Brazil.

Feijoada

For Adventurous Eaters

Bar do Mineiro, Rua Paschoal Carlos Magno, 99, Santa Teresa

Brazil's national dish is a rich, hearty black bean stew slow-cooked with pork and beef. Traditionally served on Wednesdays and Saturdays, feijoada comes with rice, collard greens, orange slices, and farofa. This dish tells the story of Brazil's culinary history and is a must-try for anyone wanting to experience authentic Rio cuisine.

Churrasco

Best for Meat Lovers

Churrascaria Palace, Rua Rodolfo Dantas, 16, Copacabana

Brazilian barbecue is an all-you-can-eat feast of perfectly grilled meats brought to your table on skewers. From picanha (top sirloin) to linguiça (sausage), the variety is incredible. Most churrascarias offer a full salad bar as well. Come hungry—this is a meal that celebrates Rio's love for good food and great company.
